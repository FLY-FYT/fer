History of Flying Vehicles

From Leonardo da Vinci (1485) to Joby Aviation (2025) — global problems and opportunities for FYT

Era 1 · Pioneers (1485–1939)
1485
Ornithopter · da Vinci
Sketches of a flapping-wing flying machine. Never built but laid the foundations of mechanized flight.
No engine, no light materials
1783
Montgolfier Balloon
First crewed human flight. Hot air. No controllable direction.
At the mercy of wind
1903
Flyer · Wright Brothers
First powered and controlled flight. 59 seconds, 260 meters. 12 HP engine.
Three-axis control principle
1907
Helicopter · Paul Cornu
First free vertical human flight. 2 meters for 20 seconds.
Unstable, hard to control
1923
Autogiro C.4 · de la Cierva
First practical rotary-wing aircraft. Direct precursor of the modern helicopter.
Autorotation rotor = safety
Era 2 · VTOL Revolution (1940–1969)
1939
Sikorsky VS-300
Modern helicopter concept: main rotor + tail rotor. Sikorsky. Standard to this day.
Config used 85 years later
1954
XFY Pogo · Convair
Tail-sitting VTOL combat aircraft. Pilot was looking skyward when landing.
Impossible to land · Cancelled
1969
Harrier · Hawker Siddeley
First practical operational VTOL jet fighter. Vectored nozzles. RAF used it for decades.
First operational VTOL jet
Era 3 · eVTOL Revolution (2010–2019)
2013
Phantom 1 · DJI
First mass-consumer drone. Gave public access to autonomous VTOL. Created the drone industry.
Democratized VTOL
2016
EHang 184
First passenger drone. 1 person. Autonomous. ~23 min flight. Only in China with restrictions.
Limited range · China only
2018
S4 · Joby Aviation
6 tilted rotors. 320 km/h. 240 km range. Most advanced in the world. Toyota & Delta invested.
Closest to FAA certification
2019
Lilium Jet
72 mini electric jets on wings. 300 km/h. 6 seats. German. SPAC 2021.
Bankrupt 2023 · Very complex tech
Era 4 · Certification Race (2020–2025)
2021-25
Joby S4 · FAA
FAA certification in progress. Deal with Delta Airlines. USAF already uses it. Closest to market.
✈️ Global reference for FYT
2023
EH216-S · EHang
World's first type certification for passenger drone. CAAC (China). Commercial flights.
✅ First certified eVTOL
2024
SA-2 · Supernal (Hyundai)
Hyundai enters eVTOL market. 5 passengers. Mass production planned with automotive infrastructure.

Global eVTOL Problems — FYT Strategic Opportunities

🔋
Battery Energy Density
Current Li-ion batteries (~250 Wh/kg) only allow 20-45 minutes of flight. >500 Wh/kg needed for practical commercial use.

💡 FYT Solution: HVDC cable from DroneTRY = unlimited range
📋
Aeronautical Certification
FAA/EASA certification takes 7-10 years and costs >$100M USD. Chile's DGAC doesn't yet have a specific framework for eVTOLs.

💡 FLY is working on this: first mover advantage
🔊
Acoustic Pollution
eVTOLs are still noisy (65-75 dB at 500m). FYT's AirCar design must incorporate noise reduction technology from TRL 3.
🏗️
Vertiport Infrastructure
No takeoff/landing infrastructure exists in cities. Santiago has none planned.

💡 FYT opportunity: alliance with developers & municipalities
🧠
Cultural Acceptance & Fear
The public fears eVTOLs falling on their homes. Fernanda's perfect strategy: educational talks with prototypes, DroneTRY cable as visible "lifeline", One-Button Fly interface.
💰
Unit Cost
Current eVTOLs cost $1-5M USD per unit. "Air taxi as a service" model (like Uber Air) can make the tech accessible without buying the vehicle. Joby already has Delta deals.
